Practice

Cardiological tests and procedures performed in the office

  • General Consultation

    A Cardiology Consultation is a comprehensive evaluation where your heart health is assessed through discussion, physical examination, and diagnostic tests. Essential for pinpointing issues and creating a tailored diagnostic and treatment plan.

  • Resting ECG

    The resting electrocardiogram (ECG) captures your heart's electrical activity and comes with an expert analysis report. Commonly performed during consultations, it's essential for diagnosing arrhythmias, blocked arteries, and other heart issues.

  • Exercise ECG

    An acquisition and analysis of electrical heart potential during exercise including a protocol and report. The most common indications for exercise ECG are chest pain, dyspnea, palpitations, syncope, preoperative evaluation, assessment of functional capacity and exercise-induced arrhythmias.

  • Echocardiography

    Echocardiography uses ultrasound to produce high-resolution images of your heart, helping to assess its size, function, and valve conditions. It's crucial for diagnosing heart disorders and guiding treatment options.

  • ECG Holter Monitoring

    Holter ECG provides continuous heart rhythm monitoring over 24-72 hours, capturing irregularities that may not show in a short-term test. Essential for diagnosing arrhythmias and unexplained cardiac symptoms

  • Arterial Blood Pressure Monitoring

    Arterial Blood Pressure Monitoring (ABPM) tracks your blood pressure over a 24-hour period, offering a comprehensive view that helps diagnose and manage hypertension and other cardiovascular conditions.
